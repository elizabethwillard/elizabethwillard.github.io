Currently, I work as a Load Forecast Data Scientist at ISO-New England, specifically focused on very-short-term day-ahead forecasts used by operations who manage the NE grid

Previously, I was an Embedded Software Engineer at IDEXX Laboratories working on the Catalyst1 and CatalystDx.

I hold a B.S. in Computer Engineering from the University of Maine and an M.S. in Data Science from UC Berkeley.

I write about whatever I'm learning or interested in. My aspirations are to become a computational neuroscientist. I've taught at Factory3 + Maine College of Art and Design (MECA). I'm working on a workshop series that extends the one class I taught at MECA that will hopefully encompass an entertaining variety of electronic stumpwork projects.
