The United States Energy Market:
- There are multiple regional ISOs but not every area of the US is covered by an ISO
- There are different structures for the energy market. Regardless of if an ISO is involved or not, the people responsible for making sure your heat can turn on need load forecasts in order to help plan for distribution and transmission upgrades. 



What exactly is load forecasting? In a nutshell, load forecasting is all about predicting the power demand for a given future period of time. There are usually two kinds of load forecasts, short-term and long-term. Short-term forecasts can be leveraged for within-day or next-day load forecasts that system operators and others rely on to ensure that energy demand is met in a given region. 

- Typically a long-term load forecast is 20-50 years into the future.
